data visuals

This section portrays my data visualisations relating to my api:

Asteroid Diameter Vs. Asteroid Name Bar Chart

This chart is a bar chart that visually represents the diameter of asteroids in meters alongside their corresponding names. The x-axis of the chart ranges from 0 to the number of asteroids for the selected date. Each asteroid's name is displayed along the y-axis, and the length of the bars extending from the x-axis to the asteroid's diameter indicates its size. The first asteroid is represented at 0, the second at 1, and so on. This chart provides a straightforward and easy-to-read visualization of the relative sizes of different asteroids on the selected date. Users can quickly compare the diameters of asteroids by looking at the length of the bars.

Distance from Earth Vs. Asteroid Name Scatterplot Chart

In this scatterplot chart, the distance of asteroids from Earth in megameters is plotted against the name of each asteroid. The x-axis ranges from 0 to the number of asteroids for the chosen date, with each point on the chart corresponding to an asteroid. The y-axis represents the distance from Earth for each asteroid. Scatterplot charts are useful for showing the distribution of data points. In this case, users can easily observe how different asteroids are distributed in terms of their distances from Earth. This chart helps users identify which asteroids are closer or farther from Earth on the selected date.

Distance from Earth Vs. Asteroid Diameter Bubble Chart

This chart combines the information from the previous two charts, displaying both the distance of asteroids from Earth and their diameters. Each asteroid is represented as a bubble on the chart. The x-axis ranges from 0 to the number of asteroids for the chosen date, just like in the other charts. The y-axis represents the distance from Earth in megameters, and the size of each bubble corresponds to the diameter of the asteroid. The larger the bubble, the larger the asteroid's diameter. By combining these two dimensions, this chart allows users to explore the relationship between the size of asteroids and their distance from Earth on the selected date. Larger bubbles indicate larger asteroids, and the position of each bubble on the chart shows how close or distant the asteroid is from Earth.
