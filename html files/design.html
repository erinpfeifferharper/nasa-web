design

This section portrays my design choices for this website:

initial wireframes

initial wireframes explaination

Home: for this page I used a Spline 3D creation I created myself, as I wanted the website to link towards the idea of asteroids, I incorporated something minimal and that looks like asteroids

About: For this page as seen in the wireframes I wanted to add more of my asteroid elements here, however it clashed with the text I intended to display about my site, so I decided to not go with it while trying to figure out a way to move around it so that it may be incorporated into the next deployment version of my site.

Data-art: This page is left blank for a reason – rubric requires it to be blank.

Data-visual: This page is left blank for a reason – rubric requires it to be blank.

Blog: For the blogs section I made a normal page section wherein you can select which blog you would like to go into, and within it are two sections that open up and display the blogs in full and on their own instead of having all of them displayed on one page which forces the reader to go through them all instead of picking which one they would like to see.

Design: this page was kept simple, just displaying my wireframes and text such as this for the user viewing to understand my reason for design choices.

initial style guide

Aestheticand Layout: For this I wanted to keep it very minimal and simple. I did not want to do too much as that can overpower the website and make it much difficult for the viewer to engage along with feeling drawn to the website.

Font: The font I use throughout my entire website was ‘Lara’ font. I love how minimal it is, however, I am open to expanding on it by using other fonts for other headings, but only if I find one that is visually appealing to my website and me.

Colour Scheme: As seen from most of the information along with digital art interactions displayed the colour scheme for this is shades of grey. The reason as to why I used shades of grey is to represent the asteroids and their colour, another reason being, I want my data visualisations and art to match the colour scheme of my website, however I need to first structure my data out and figure the colour scheme I intend to use there before I decide on one.

updated wireframes

updated wireframes explaination

Home: for this page I initially used a Spline 3D creation I created myself, however from the feedback received in the first assignment, my creation did not display as it mentioned that I needed to add something to it. After which I found a way to incorporate a spline design as it requires the design to be static instead of moving. The only problem with using a spline design is the loading time for it has not been imported as a normal image.

About: For this page, I changed my whole idea of how I wanted it to be displayed, as I incorporated more colour and blocks separating the two information instead of having it just be separated without having people being able to differentiate between the two.

Data-art: In this section, I have decided to incorporate a feature that lets the user decide on what date they would like to see data on, and it will automatically generate the information after they have chosen the date and selected the generation key. I wanted to create a giant circle/orbit with asteroids on them to create a data artwork, however, I created only orbits with different colours, I want the first orbit being the smallest to have the lightest colour with the last orbit being the largest to have the darkest colour. Thereafter when you hover over the orbit you will get the information about it, the smallest orbit represents the asteroid closest to Earth and the largest represents the asteroid furthest from Earth. In the recent artwork (being the second one), I decided to create an artwork that represents network art. For this I wanted the lines to display the distance between each asteriod, however due to my api being very limited, I could not add that feature.

Data-visual: In this wireframe section, I have decided to incorporate a feature that lets the user decide on what date they would like to see data on, and it will automatically generate the information after they have chosen the date and selected the generation key. So as the user enters there will be nothing but after they generate one, two data visualizations will appear in different graph formats. One will display data regarding the diameter of the asteroid (in metres) and the other the distance of the asteroid from earth (in megametres). Furthermore, I have added the updaed visuals of the wireframes and what kind of graph I would like to create for my thrid graph - which is an interactive graph.

Blog: For the blogs section, I kept it like my initial blogs, however I added images to make it look more interesting and aesthetically pleasing. Furthermore, I worked on how information in the blog sections were displayed, I made them stand out more compared to the background while making necessary changes to the various font sizes and removing ‘br’ tags that were used to create space.

Design: this page was kept the same as it just displayed my wireframes and text such as this for the user viewing to understand my reason for design choices. However it was modified to look much better than before. This section also includes my previous wireframes and their notes along with the new updated ones.

updated style guide

Aesthetic and Layout: As previously mentioned, I wanted to keep the layout and aesthetics of my website very minimal, however seeing that previous on was too pink, i made it grey and link more to Asteriods.

Font: The font I use throughout my entire website was ‘Lara’ font, which has not changed. I love how minimal it is and plan to continue using it.

Colour Scheme: The colour scheme I have chosen to go with is shades of grey with hints of pink, purple and blue in the visuals of my data art and data visuals pages. Therefore breaking away from the pink I had in my website initially, as I was told it does not show the theme of the NASA API.
